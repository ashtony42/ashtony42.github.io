Healthcare

"The COVID-19 pandemic has further demonstrated that healthcare is a human right; nobody should have to go into debt because they or their loved ones become sick."

Single payer healthcare for all Ohioans

  • Medical debt is the number one reason that Ohioans are forced to declare bankruptcy. Single Payer (or Medicare for All) would eliminate out-of-pocket costs for healthcare.

  • Ohio spends $81.6 billion a year on healthcare, largely due to costs associated with healthcare administration and private insurance. National analyses project approximately 75% cuts in these costs under Medicare for All.

  • Medicare for All would curb outrageous price-gouging from drug companies and allow the state to negotiate affordable prices on drugs and treatment instead of profit-driven corporations and insurance providers.

Ensuring availability of COVID-19 resources

  • Regardless of income, insurance, or housing status, everybody should have easy access to effective and free COVID-19 vaccines.

  • We must protect and promote resources for medical, economic, and emotional recovery from the pandemic, such as unemployment payments for those missing work and support systems for those who have been diagnosed and/or suffered from losses of loved ones.

Holding the pharmaceutical industry accountable for the opioid crisis

  • Since the 1990s, pharmaceutical companies have distributed opioids through sales tactics rather than based on need. The resulting addiction epidemic has cost countless lives and $78.5 billion per year.

  • As with the tobacco industry, we must hold this industry financially and legally accountable and invest into addiction treatments to help those struggling to recover.
