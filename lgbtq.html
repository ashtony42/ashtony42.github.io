LGBTQ+ Rights

LGBTQ+ Ohioans deserve equal rights and protections under the law as all others.

Defending LGBTQ+ Livelihoods

  • LGBTQ+ Ohioans deserve to live without fear of losing their jobs, homes, health, or dignity because of their identity.

  • The Supreme Court ruled in July 2020 that discrimination of the basis of sexual or gender identity in housing or employment is unconstitutional. We must revisit Chapter 4112 of the OhioÂ Revised Code to include specific language within our state laws to reflect this ruling.

  • We must also pass the Ohio Equality Act to expand these protections to include housing, education, and healthcare in the wake of recent federal decisions that have undermined these rights, particularly for transgender Americans.

Protecting LGBTQ+ Minors

  • The American Psychiatric Association has unanimously agreed that conversion therapy is extremely harmful for the wellbeing of LGBTQ+ people.

  • We will reintroduce and pass Ohio Senate Bill 74, which bans the practice of conversion therapy on minors and has sat dead in committee since 2015.

Ensuring Legal Recognition of Gender Identities

  • Intersex and non-binary Ohioans have limited options when it comes to appropriate gender markers on state documents.

  • We must pass legislation allowing for neutral gender designations on state IDs, driver's licenses, and marriage certificates.
