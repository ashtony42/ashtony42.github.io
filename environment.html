Ban Fracking

  • Fracking involves injecting large quantities of chemicals directly into the earth to extract oil and natural gases within, a process with many environmental consequences, including contamination of drinking water, which has a reversal process that been deemed too costly to implement.

  • In 2016, Ohio's Secretary of State (now Lieutenant Governor) Jon Husted threw out ballot measures in Athens, Meigs, and Portage counties that would have banned fracking locally had they been approved by voters. Not only is this an environmentally dangerous decision, it was an act by our state government that undermined Ohioans' rights to self-govern.

Curtail Water Pollution

  • Lake Erie is the most polluted of the Great Lakes, which are the largest source of fresh water in the entire world.

  • Algae blooms, which were once a rare occurrence, are now regularly expected due to pollutants from fertilizer and manure runoff from farmland.

  • We can encourage the agricultural industry to cut back on environmentally harmful practices through small changes such as tax incentives for small, family-run farms, farmers who utilize phosphorus-free fertilizers, and property owners who install rain collection barrels
